The "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" Threat

Waiting for "Q-Day" is a losing strategy. "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" (HNDL) is an active, state-level reality. Adversaries are stockpiling encrypted blockchain data today, knowing that cryptographically relevant quantum computers will eventually break elliptic-curve cryptography.

For privacy coins relying on legacy Monero-style cryptography, the math is unforgiving. A quantum break means retroactive de-anonymization of the entire historical ledger and silent supply inflation.

The Reality Check:

  • The 2026 Thales Data Threat Report cites HNDL as the top security concern for 61% of organizations.
  • Citi Institute warns of trillions in potential economic damage from a single quantum breach.
  • Data harvested today is permanently vulnerable unless the network migrates immediately.
